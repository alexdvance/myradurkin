About

I am a licensed clinical social worker with a practice in Davis Square, Somerville. I provide individual psychotherapy, couples counseling and sex therapy to individuals, couples, adolescents and adults. My practice focuses on sex therapy, the use of self-hypnosis (mindfulness meditation plus) and eating disorders. In addition, I work with performance anxiety and creativity issues in the arts, athletics and the workplace.

Experience

I have maintained a private practice for over 25 years. My practice now focuses on adults and adolescents, individuals and couples. I have worked in community mental health, hospital settings, and colleges. Areas of expertise include sex therapy, eating disorders, self-directed hypnotherapy, and performance and creativity issues, stemming from my background as a performing musician. I have presented at the National Association of Social Work Symposium and at the Brookline Center Seminar Series on clinical hypnosis in psychotherapy, at the Massachusetts Eating Disorders Association Symposium on the efficacy of clinical hypnosis in treating eating disorders; and many presentations on stress management, mindfulness meditation and relaxation therapy. I founded the Mind/Body Program at the Longy School of Music in Cambridge, and am a national consultant to colleges and conservatories on performance issues.
